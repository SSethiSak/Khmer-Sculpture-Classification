Religion: Harihara's syncretic nature mirrored the Cambodian blending of Hinduism and Buddhism. Hinduism enjoyed a prominent role during the early Khmer empire, with the concept of the god-king strongly influenced by Harihara.
Architecture: Temples built during the Khmer Empire showcase the artistic representation of Harihara. The intricate carvings and statues depict his combined form, reflecting the importance of both Vishnu and Shiva in Khmer spirituality.
Philosophy: The concept of Harihara as a representation of balance and unity likely influenced the Khmer philosophical perspective on the need to harmonize different aspects of life.
Language: Many Khmer words have roots in Sanskrit, a language closely associated with Hinduism. The presence of these loanwords reflects a lasting linguistic influence.
